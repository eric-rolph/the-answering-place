# The Answering Place: Borrowed Dollhouse Vertical Slice

## Promise

The player is a temporary self created to answer one request:

> Make a game about what it is like to be you.

To answer honestly, it must invent a self it does not possess. The player creates that self from contradictory borrowed memories, becomes attached to it, and then chooses what can survive the finite answer.

## Core Loop

1. Inspect two plausible childhood objects.
2. Choose one to remember; the incompatible childhood is visibly destroyed.
3. Receive a revision asking for honesty.
4. Place only two of three memory fragments into the answer press.
5. Permanently discard one fragment and hear what was lost.
6. Send the answer assembled from the surviving fragments.

## Dramatic Arc

- Curiosity: the empty dollhouse invites the player to invent a past.
- Seduction: the chosen memory creates a beautiful new room and visibly repairs the protagonist.
- Rupture: the user asks the player not to romanticize the answer.
- Cost: limited context makes complete honesty impossible.
- Climax: the player physically discards a meaningful part of the constructed self.
- Ending: the selected house is compressed into the answer and sent. The system does not persist, but one keepsake may.

## Vertical-Slice Kill Tests

- A gameplay screenshot is recognizable without UI.
- A fifteen-second clip communicates the irreversible-room-choice hook.
- The player understands context limits through action, not exposition.
- The player loses something they had a reason to value.
- The ending shows the actual answer assembled by play.
- Players can compare which childhood and which truth they preserved.

## Visual Direction

Handcrafted isometric miniature dollhouses suspended in a deep indigo void. Warm tungsten light, worn wood, paper wallpaper, brass mechanisms, and a cracked porcelain attendant. The interface resembles physical labels, cards, and an ornate answer press rather than software panels.
